Project Overview
This project performs exploratory data analysis (EDA) on New York City motor vehicle collision data from January to August 2020. The goal is to identify temporal trends, high-risk periods, and key contributing factors behind road accidents, with a focus on generating data-driven safety insights.

Dataset
Source: NYPD Motor Vehicle Collisions (Open Data)
Time Period: Jan 2020 â€“ Aug 2020
Scope: All reported motor vehicle collisions within New York City

Objectives
-Analyze monthly and hourly accident trends
-Identify peak accident times and high-risk periods
-Examine contributing factors behind collisions
-Compare patterns between fatal and non-fatal accidents

Key Insights
-Accidents peak during weekday rush hours (8â€“10 AM, 4â€“7 PM)
-Overall accident volume dropped during COVID lockdowns, but severity ratios increased
-Driver inattention/distraction was the most common contributing factor
-Speeding and failure to yield were more strongly associated with fatal crashes

Results & Impact
The findings highlight how traffic volume, driver behavior, and time of day influence accident frequency and severity. These insights can support targeted enforcement, policy planning, and road safety initiatives.
